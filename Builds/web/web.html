Once, We Stood in the Rain:

You are a governer or manager of a department of a certain city. It is far in the future. All rain has become acidic. The City must undertake measures to protect its buildings and citizens from this weather phenomenon. Utilize tax income to erect rain shields. Manage them wisely. Covering up too much of the city may lead to unhappiness, buildup of pollution, and decreased effectiveness of industry. Not protecting the city will cause both up-front damage and injuries as well as irreversible harm to the soil. Butt heads with corporations who refuse to comply with your orders. How will your city be zoned? Can you protect everyone, or will there be sacrifices for the greater prosperity of the city? As time passes, the rain worsens... see how long your city can last.
